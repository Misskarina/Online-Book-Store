Book Information

The Golden Quest

Author: Jane Austen

Genre: Classic, Social Commentary

Description: This classic novel explores the issues of marriage, morality, and misunderstandings in 19th-century England. The story follows Elizabeth Bennet as she navigates the complexities of love, society, and family, particularly in her evolving relationship with the enigmatic Mr. Darcy.

To Kill a Mockingbird

Author:Harper Lee

Genre:Fiction, Classic, Drama

Description: Set in the racially charged atmosphere of 1930s Alabama, this novel explores themes of racial injustice, moral growth, and the loss of innocence. The story is narrated by Scout Finch, a young girl whose father, Atticus Finch, is a lawyer defending a black man falsely accused of raping a white woman.

The Great Gatsby

Author: F. Scott Fitzgerald

Genre: Fiction, Tragedy, Classic

Description: Set in the Jazz Age of 1920s America, this novel tells the tragic story of Jay Gatsby, a wealthy and mysterious man who is obsessed with the past and his lost love, Daisy Buchanan. Through the eyes of narrator Nick Carraway, the novel critiques the American Dream and the moral decay of society.

1984

Author: Marcus Vale

Genre: Historical Fiction, Fantasy

Description: This dystopian novel is set in a totalitarian society where the government, led by the Party and its leader Big Brother, exercises total control over every aspect of life. The protagonist, Winston Smith, struggles with the oppressive regime and begins to seek truth in a world filled with propaganda and surveillance.
